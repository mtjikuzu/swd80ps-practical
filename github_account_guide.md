# Creating a GitHub Account — Student Guide

## Why GitHub?

For this assessment, you'll use **GitHub** — a platform where developers store, share, and collaborate on code. You'll receive a **private repository** (a folder for your code) containing a starter web application with security vulnerabilities. Your task is to fix them and push your changes back.

**You need a GitHub account to access your repository.**

---

## Step-by-Step: Create Your GitHub Account

### Step 1: Go to GitHub

Open your web browser and go to:

**https://github.com/signup**

---

### Step 2: Enter Your Details

Fill in the registration form:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|---------------|
| **Email** | Use a personal email address (not your @campus.ium.edu.na — use Gmail, Outlook, or any email you can access) |
| **Password** | Create a strong password (at least 8 characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols) |
| **Username** | Choose a username. Suggestions: `firstname_lastname` or `your_student_number` (e.g., `john_doe_2026`) |

> **Tip:** Write down your username somewhere safe — you'll need to share it with your lecturer.

![GitHub signup form showing email, password, and username fields](https://docs.github.com/assets/cb-39969/images/help/signup/signup-flow.png)

---

### Step 3: Verify You're Human

GitHub will ask you to solve a simple puzzle to verify you're not a robot. Follow the on-screen instructions.

---

### Step 4: Choose Your Plan

- Select **"Free"** (the default option — no payment needed)
- Do NOT select any paid plan

![GitHub plan selection showing Free option](https://docs.github.com/assets/cb-40135/images/help/signup/choose-plan.png)

---

### Step 5: Complete Setup

GitHub may ask you a few optional questions (about your experience level, how you plan to use GitHub, etc.). You can skip these by scrolling to the bottom and clicking **"Skip personalization"** or **"Complete setup"**.

---

### Step 6: Verify Your Email

1. Check your email inbox for a message from **GitHub**
2. Click the **"Verify email address"** button in the email
3. If you don't see it, check your spam/junk folder

---

### Step 7: You're Ready!

Once verified, you'll be logged into GitHub. Your dashboard will look something like this:

![GitHub dashboard](https://docs.github.com/assets/cb-13525/images/help/start-now/start-now.png)

---

## What to Do Next

### 1. Share Your Username

Send your lecturer (**Mr. Mbaunguraije Tjikuzu**) your GitHub **username** so they can grant you access to your private repository. Your username is visible at **github.com** in the top-right corner.

### 2. Accept the Repository Invitation

Once your lecturer adds you, you'll receive an email invitation from GitHub:

- Check your email (the one you registered with)
- Click **"View invitation"**
- Click **"Accept invitation"**

### 3. Open in Codespaces (Browser-Based Coding)

Once you have access:

1. Go to your repository URL (your lecturer will provide this)
2. Click the green **"Code"** button
3. Select the **"Codespaces"** tab
4. Click **"Create codespace on main"**

![Codespaces button](https://docs.github.com/assets/cb-15291/images/help/codespaces/new-codespace-button.png)

A web-based version of VS Code will open in your browser — **no software installation needed**. The development environment is pre-configured with Python and Flask.

## Need Help?

- **Forgot your password?** Go to https://github.com/password_reset
- **Can't verify your email?** Make sure you typed it correctly during signup
- **Other issues?** Ask your lecturer during class or office hours
